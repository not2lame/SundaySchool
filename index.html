Background: Deepening Doctrine and Increasing Trials in Nauvoo

The revelations in this lesson were given during the latter part of the Nauvoo period, a time of significant doctrinal development for the Church. Having established a thriving city, the Saints were receiving profound new truths through the Prophet Joseph Smith. The Lord was revealing the nature of God, the reality of post-mortal life, and the eternal potential of families. These revelations brought heaven closer, making eternal life feel both glorious and attainable.

However, this period of enlightenment was also a time of severe testing. The Lord restored the ancient practice of plural marriage, a commandment that was a deep trial of faith for Joseph, his wife Emma, and nearly everyone who was asked to live it. This principle, which is no longer practiced by the Church, required a level of faith and sacrifice that went far beyond comfort or convenience. The revelations in this lesson, therefore, come from a context of both sublime truth and immense personal sacrifice, teaching us about God's laws and the faith required to obey them.

God wants to exalt His children.

Discussion Point:

While we don't know everything about exaltation, the Lord has revealed precious truths that help us understand our divine potential. We learn that God the Father "has a body of flesh and bones as tangible as man's". We also learn about the nature of heaven, where the same "sociality which exists among us here" will continue, coupled with eternal glory. These doctrines demystify eternity and help us see exaltation as a continuation of our most cherished relationships.

Doctrine and Covenants 130:2: "And that same sociality which exists among us here will exist among us there, only it will be coupled with eternal glory, which glory we do not now enjoy."

Discussion Question:

How does knowing that God has a tangible body and that our righteous relationships and sociality will continue in heaven affect the way you view your life and relationships right now?

Possible Answers: It gives deeper meaning to our daily lives. It motivates us to build strong, loving relationships based on Christlike principles, knowing they can last forever. It helps us see our physical bodies as sacred gifts, created in the image of God, and not just temporary shells. It makes heaven feel less distant and more like a loving home we are preparing to return to.

God blesses people who obey His laws.

Discussion Point:

The Lord reveals a fundamental principle of heaven: all blessings are predicated upon obedience to divine law. This is an eternal and irrevocable decree. When we receive any blessing from God, it is because we have been obedient to the law upon which that blessing is based. This principle applies to all of God's commandments.

Doctrine and Covenants 130:20-21: "There is a law, irrevocably decreed in heaven before the foundations of this world, upon which all blessings are predicated‚ÄîAnd when we obtain any blessing from God, it is by obedience to that law upon which it is predicated."

Discussion Question:

Sometimes we are obedient, yet the specific blessings we hope for are delayed. How does the principle- taught in these verses help you maintain faith when blessings don't come on your expected timeline?

Possible Answers: This principle teaches us to trust in the law itself, not just the timing. We can have faith that the law is unchangeable and that blessings will come, even if it's in the Lord's time and way. It shifts our focus from demanding a specific outcome to focusing on our own consistent obedience, trusting that God will always keep His promises.

Heavenly Father made it possible for families to be eternal.

Discussion Point:

The Lord reveals the new and everlasting covenant of marriage, clarifying the conditions required for a family to be eternal. A marriage must be performed by proper priesthood authority ("by him who is anointed"), sealed by the Holy Spirit of Promise, and the couple must abide in that covenant. Any marriage not meeting these conditions will have an end when the partners die. Only those who meet the Lord's conditions will be "sealed for time and for all eternity" and can become "gods, because they have no end."

Doctrine and Covenants 132:19: "And again, verily I say unto you, if a man marry a wife by my word, which is my law, and by the new and everlasting covenant, and it is sealed unto them by the Holy Spirit of promise... it shall be of full force when they are out of the world..."

Discussion Question:

President Eyring shared counsel he received: "You just live worthy of the celestial kingdom, and the family arrangements will be more wonderful than you can imagine." How does this counsel bring hope and direction, especially for those whose current family situations are complex or not ideal?

Possible Answers: This counsel shifts the focus from circumstances we can't control to the one thing we can: our personal worthiness. It offers immense hope that the Lord, in His infinite wisdom and love, will ensure that all who are faithful will receive every promised blessing of an eternal family. It allows us to have peace, trusting that He will make things right in a way "more wonderful than [we] can imagine."

Plural marriage is acceptable to God only when He commands it.

Discussion Point:

Joseph Smith had questions about the Old Testament practice of plural marriage. In response, the Lord revealed that He, the Lord, commanded Abraham, Jacob, and others to enter into plural marriage. While the standard law for marriage is between one man and one woman, there have been times when God has commanded the practice as an exception. The early history of the restored Church was one of those exceptional periods. The revelation makes it clear that plural marriage is only acceptable when authorized and commanded by God through His prophet.

Doctrine and Covenants 132:34: "God commanded Abraham, and Sarah gave Hagar to Abraham to wife. And why did she do it? Because this was the law; and from Hagar sprang many people. This, therefore, was fulfilling, among other things, the promises."

Discussion Question:

What does the Lord‚Äôs instruction on plural marriage teach you about the nature of God‚Äôs commandments and our responsibility to follow the living prophet?

Possible Answers: It teaches that God's laws are constant, but His specific instructions to His people can vary according to His will and purposes. It reinforces the critical importance of following the current prophet. We are not bound by past commands that have been rescinded. Our safety and blessings lie in obeying what the Lord has revealed for His Church today through His chosen mouthpiece.

Summary of the Lesson

This lesson delves into profound doctrines revealed in Nauvoo. We learn about the glorious nature of exaltation, where we can become like our Heavenly Father, who has a tangible body, and where our righteous family relationships can continue forever. The lesson reinforces the eternal principle that all blessings from God are based on obedience to His laws. A central focus is the new and everlasting covenant of eternal marriage, which, when sealed by proper authority, allows families to be together forever. Finally, the lesson explains the principle of plural marriage as a commandment given by God in ancient times and as an exception during the early Restoration, emphasizing that it was only practiced when commanded by the Lord through His prophet.
